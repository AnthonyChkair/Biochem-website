Overview

Enzymes are biological catalysts that increase the rate of chemical reactions without being consumed. They are usually proteins and exhibit high specificity for their substrates.

Key Points

  • Lower activation energy
  • Do not change reaction equilibrium
  • Can be regulated by inhibitors and activators
